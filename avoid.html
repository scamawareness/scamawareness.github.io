How do you avoid scams?

Scammers rely on getting the victim to make irrational decisions, in order to manipulate you into giving sensitive information, or money.
                     
First off, know the tricks of the scammer.
  • Urgency - Terms such as "Last Chance", "Limited Offer" are used to make the victim act quickly, which often leads to irrational decisions (which then lead to a victory for the scammer.)
  • Threats - Scammers will often pose as a government organisation and threaten you with fines, court, arrest, and deportation. The victim is then expected to panick, and make irrational decisions. 
  • Confidence Trick - Scammers will claim to be professionals (doctors, lawyers, investors) in order to earn confidence while carrying out the scam. The victim is led to trust the scammers, and follow their advice and commands.
  • Fake Identities - Scammers will create fake identites and social media profiles in order interact with the victim and offer scam services, or, they will use the fake identities to play confidence tricks and romance scams (where the victim is convinced to fall in love with the scammer).
Now you have a list of what to look for, here are some good practises to avoid scammers.
  1. Keep sensitive information to yourself - Never share sensitive information with parties that you don't trust.
  2. Check bank and credit card statements - This is a good practise to perform monthly, in order to monitor suspicious activity.
  3. Signups - During signups for websites that you don't trust, never share any sensitive information, and use a throwaway alternate email.
  4. Payment Method - Certain payment methods have little to none fraud protection, and are more favourable for scammers: reloadable cards, gift cards, and wiring money through Western Union and MoneyGram. Trustworthy companies will (almost) never use these methods.
  5. Question  - when in phone calls, ask for the name of the CEO, your address, and the
    name + company of the caller.
If it's too good to be true, it probably isn't.

TL;DR
Know what scammers use to scam, be wary and do some routine checks, and question things.
