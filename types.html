Types of Scams

Romance Scams

Romance scams most commonly happen on dating websites, and take advantage of people searching for romantic partners, by posing as possible partners.

 The scammers then play on emotional triggers and make requests for money, services, and other things of value.

    Unexpected Money Scam

Also a part of the "get-rich scheme" scams, scammers play the victim into paying a fee or giving up sensitive information in exchange to collect a prize or get access to the money-making method.

Threats

Scammers will threaten you with death, hacking, or other forms of blackmail in order to extort money.

Sometimes, scammers will also choose to pose as a governmental group and threaten fines, arrest, and deportation in exchange for money. (ATO scam)

Phishing

Scammers will pose as trustworthy entities and make seemingly genuine emails, which alert of account deactivations and overdue payments.

The scammers will often then link to a website that appears almost identical to the genuine website, and require a login or other form of signup. The scammer then acquires your sensitive information.
