The internet has become a favourable platform for fraud, as a result of the anonymity and wide variety of vulnerable victims.

The internet is a well-known force on the world we currently live in, but also makes us vulnerable to scams and fraud. For example, the conversion of money to digital makes money transfer easier than ever before, but also makes it easier for us to misplace money into the hands of scammers.

Out of the 100 million dollars that was lost from scams during 2018, almost 80% of that was lost online.

Additionally, the internet makes it easier to find vulnerable victims and manipulate information and identity to gain the confidence of victims.

The Growth of Online Scaming

Online scamming is a cybercrime that's quickly gaining in popularity, likely as a result of rapid developments in internet access, as well as many other factors:

Anonymity

The anonymity of the internet minimizes the risk of the scammer being caught, and can be reduced even further via VPN.

Spam

Phishing emails and other attempts to scam can be sent to thousands of people, simultaneously, with ease.

Scammers only need a few successful scams to make massive profits.

Identity

Identity theft and impersonation is easy on the internet, under the mask of anonymity.

In addition, there are countless techniques scammers have access to as a result of the internet.

  • Phishing scams, which rely on imitating a reputable company in order to gain confidence and retrieve personal information, money, and other services.
  • "Prize" scams, which rely on naivety and almost always lead to you needing to pay a fee in order to collect the prize.
  • Romance scams, or "catfishing" as a lover to earn confidence, often leading to the scammer asking for "gifts" and "loans". (often works most successfully on vulnerable victims, which are easily found through the internet.)
  • Typical confidence tricks, which rely on authority. i.e. impersonating or pretending to be a doctor, lawyer, investor, nigerian prince, rich CEO, etc.
